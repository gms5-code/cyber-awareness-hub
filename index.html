Live Video Cloning
A practical awareness page on real-time deepfake video threats, their impact on business communications, and how to reduce the risk.
What are real-time deepfakes?

Real-time deepfake video uses AI to alter a person's face, voice, or gestures during a live video session. The effect can be convincing enough to impersonate a manager, a colleague, or a trusted contact in a business call.

Why this is a security risk

Attackers can use this technique to pressure employees, trigger urgent actions, or bypass normal verification. In enterprise environments, the highest-risk scenarios are urgent payment requests, confidential disclosures, and fake executive instructions during video calls.

How to spot or reduce the risk

Watch for unnatural blinking, delayed lip-sync, inconsistent lighting, unusual background behavior, or sudden changes in the person's speaking pattern. Protect yourself by using secondary verification channels, confirming requests through a trusted contact, and treating high-impact requests as sensitive even in video communication.

Awareness takeaway

Video alone is no longer enough to prove identity. Awareness, verification procedures, and cautious behavior remain the best defenses against live AI-driven impersonation.

What is AI Voice Cloning?
Modern AI can transform any voice into another in real time. Attackers use this technology to impersonate executives, colleagues, or trusted contacts in phone calls and voice messages — bypassing human verification entirely. This module lets you experience it firsthand: record a few seconds of your voice, pick a target identity, and hear what an attacker could sound like.
